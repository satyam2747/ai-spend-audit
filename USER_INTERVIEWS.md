# User Interviews & Validation

Before building the Audit Engine, we spoke with 3 startup founders to understand their pain points around AI tooling.

## Interview 1: Alex (CTO, Pre-seed SaaS)
- **Team Size**: 4
- **Current Stack**: ChatGPT Plus (all 4), GitHub Copilot (all 4).
- **Pain Point**: "I feel like Copilot is getting worse, and my devs just keep ChatGPT open in another window. We are paying $30/user/mo."
- **Insight**: They didn't know Cursor existed. Moving to Cursor saves them $10/user and gives them a better workflow.

## Interview 2: Sarah (Founder, Marketing Agency)
- **Team Size**: 8
- **Current Stack**: ChatGPT Team, Midjourney, Jasper.
- **Pain Point**: "I signed up for ChatGPT Team because I thought we needed the data privacy, but half the team still uses their personal free accounts. We also pay for Jasper but nobody logs into it anymore."
- **Insight**: Huge redundancy. Jasper is ghost-ware. ChatGPT Team is being underutilized.

## Interview 3: David (Solo Founder)
- **Team Size**: 1
- **Current Stack**: ChatGPT Plus, Claude Pro, Perplexity Pro.
- **Pain Point**: "I have FOMO. I want the best model so I subscribe to all of them, but my credit card bill is $60/mo just for chat interfaces."
- **Insight**: Solo founders need a recommendation on the *one* tool that provides the best all-around value to cut redundant subscriptions.

## Conclusion
The problem of "AI SaaS Sprawl" is real, acute, and easy to fix with basic rule-based logic.
