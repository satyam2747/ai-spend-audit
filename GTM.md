# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

## Target Audience
- **Primary**: Pre-seed and Seed-stage startup founders.
- **Secondary**: Heads of Engineering or Operations at small companies (10-50 employees).

## Problem Statement
Startups adopted AI tools rapidly over the last year. Now, they are suffering from "SaaS Sprawl." They pay for ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, and random AI marketing tools for the whole team, resulting in thousands of dollars in hidden monthly burn.

## Acquisition Channels
1. **Product Hunt Launch**: Launching as a free tool for founders to audit their stack. "Are you burning money on AI? Find out in 60 seconds."
2. **Twitter/X Indie Hacker Community**: Sharing "Shocking stats on how much startups waste on redundant AI."
3. **SEO**: Targeting long-tail keywords like:
   - "ChatGPT Team vs Plus for startups"
   - "Is Claude Pro worth it"
   - "Reduce SaaS spend AI tools"
4. **Cold Email**: Using the tool itself as a wedge. Sending cold emails to founders offering a free, custom AI audit report.

## The Funnel
1. **Top of Funnel**: User lands on SpendAudit.com.
2. **Activation**: User takes the 60-second quiz (Zero friction, no login required).
3. **The Hook**: User sees their "Total Potential Savings" blurred out.
4. **Conversion (Lead Capture)**: User enters their email to get the detailed breakdown and the Claude-generated summary.
5. **Retention/Upsell**: (Future) Offer a paid service to actually negotiate/consolidate these tools on their behalf, or sell the leads to SaaS management platforms like Vendr or Ramp.
